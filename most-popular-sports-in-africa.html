Most popular sports in Africa

Publish date: 2024-07-31

When it comes to sports, beating African nations is not easy. The entire continent is bound together by their unconditional love for sports. In fact, sports have been a key part of the African mainland for eons now. African players can be found in almost every type of sport played today, and they are not backing out anytime soon!

The African continent is renowned for producing the best sportsmen and athletes that have dominated their respective sports fields for decades. These players have risen to set world records or break the old ones. Either way, sportsmen and women from Africa are paving the way for future athletes to follow in their footsteps and dominate almost every sport there is.

In the terms of sports facilities, the nations leave no stones unturned. You will find the best sports and recreational avenues and courses in all corners of the continent. Some of the most prevalent sports in Africa include:

Regarding football, you do not require a lot of resources – just a ball, two sticks for each goal, and the players. Therefore, this sport has become very trendy in Africa. In fact, Africa organizes the African Cup of Nations each year to bring out the best football talents from the continent. Some famous African-descent footballers include Samuel Eto’o, Didier Drogba, etc.

Rugby Union

Rugby did not show any growth potential when it was first kicked off in the continent. Soon, the tides started turning, and rugby started getting recognition in different African states like Ghana and Kenya. Based on a Sunbet review, there are approximately 60,000 registered rugby players in the present day. On the global charts, South Africa ranks in the 5th spot. Additionally, the National Rugby team of Kenya won the World Series held in Dubai.

Running

African nations have witnessed the rise of several well-known athletic powerhouses that have dominated field events in almost every Olympics today. Even today, athletes from Africa are setting world records in these sports events. Some of the best athletes include Catherine Ndereba, David Rudisha, and Reggie Walker.

Cricket

When you take a look at the international cricket charts, you will always see Zimbabwe, Kenya, and South Africa within the top ten cricket teams in the international circuit. This is no small feat. In fact, these three nations also co-hosted the ICC World Cup in 2003.

Basketball

When we watch an NBA game, we see a lot of players of African descent. There is a very good explanation for this. Basketball is quite a popular sport here. Most schools participate in basketball competitions and it is a loved sport among all, from young to old! NBA players like Dikembe Mutombo and Hakeem Olajuwon can be credited to be some of the most renowned basketball pros from the continent.
